What is Website Development?

Website development is the process of us creating your website with the help of computer languages, frameworks, and other tools. It involves making and managing the back-end and front-end parts of a website so it works, is easy to use, and can be reached by anyone. Where website design emphasizes the experience of the user (the person visiting your website), web development focuses on the site’s functionality across every aspect. 

Our team of skilled website developers excels in the following web development disciplines.

  1. Front-end web development
  2. Back-end development
  3. Full-stack development

Our Website Development Process

Our web development process helps align development resources, your company goals, and your team members to ensure all aspects of the project are addressed and launched on time.

Here are the steps in our website design and development process:

  1. Research
  2. Information architecture
  3. Wireframes
  4. Design
  5. Content
  6. Development
  7. Quality analysis
  8. Launch

Research

Research is the first phase of our web development cycle. It includes:

  1. Initial research
  2. Information gathering
  3. Expectations

During our evaluation process, we'll figure out what features your site needs to have to maximize revenue and meet your business requirements. During this phase, we look more closely at each of these features to confirm their scope and find any other needs that weren't talked about or listed in the initial proposal.

Information Architecture

Information architecture is the structure by which the information on your website will be organized. By mapping everything out, we will help you visualize how and where content and information appear on your website.

We make sure your site is structured for both users and search engines by evaluating and potentially restructuring the information architecture, and we will develop a roadmap for how to build and structure the site moving forward.

Wireframes

Once the required page templates have been identified, we proceed to wireframes. Wireframes are "back of the napkin" sketches depicting the structure of a page template. It employs black-and-white page elements to provide a sense of where page components will be placed on a page so that users can react and adjust as necessary.

The Importance of UX and UI Design

UX and UI design are crucial to the success of any website. When creating a responsive design with a unique look, our UX designers refer to your brand guidelines in order to ensure all visual elements, including colors, logos, graphics, and more, are perfectly cohesive with your company's messaging, identity, and overall aesthetic.

Taking the time to make sure the website is easy to navigate and all design elements reflect your brand will help craft a seamless user experience your customers can easily identify and interact with.

Web Design

When approaching website design, we consider your target audience. Through a series of workshops that reveal more about what your customers want and need, we work with you to figure out how to create the best user experience and interface design.

We make sure to fine-tune your site's information architecture and write compelling messaging that grabs users' attention and shows what your brand is all about.

We use best practices for search engine optimization (SEO) to raise the profile and visibility of your company in search results.

Design elements are carefully chosen to give the site a unique look and make it easy to find content and products. Back-end and front-end design work together to make sure the whole site runs smoothly.

Creating Content

As our team creates wireframes and designs, it’s time to get the content ready for injection into your new site design.

Content consists of copy, images, graphics, or other design elements. Our team can handle content creation, or can work with your existing marketing team to ensure that the content they create is aligned with our shared goals.

To better organize your content, we create a content plan that includes each page on the site that needs to be loaded, links to relevant content and creative deliverables, and status columns to monitor content management and loading.

Web Development

Web development includes web design but goes far beyond it. Here are some of the important subdisciplines our professional web developers work within:

  • Database architecture involves database management systems like MySQL, MongoDB, or PostgreSQL to design and implement database structures and integrations that power your website.
  • Front-end web development is when HTML and CSS and JavaScript are used to make the parts of your website that your users interact with.
  • Back-end development involves a programming language of choice like PHP, Python, Ruby, or Java to build the logic that runs the store on the server side.
  • Full-stack development means working on both the front-end and back-end parts of the website.
  • Web security is the process of keeping tech sites safe from attacks, data leaks, and other security risks. Web security must be integrated into the web development process.
  • Performance optimization is the process of making websites and web apps run faster and work better by optimizing code, shrinking file sizes, using caching, and other methods.
  • Content management systems (CMS) let you control the content and functionality of online stores using platforms like WordPress.
  • Transactional development involves the integration of payment gateways, subscriptions, and other tech features to enable transfer of funds, delivery of services, and other activities necessary to ecommerce store or other online business.

Security, site speed, third-party integrations, search engine optimization, and flexibility are paramount for mature businesses. Our designers and developers create custom solutions tailored to address these needs and more. We pair website development with a focus on branding, design, and conversions.

Web Development vs. Web Design

Both web development and web design involve making a website, but there are important differences between the two. Let’s take a closer look at the difference between web development and web design:

Web Designers:

  • Our web designers design everything a user encounters when using a website or piece of software. This includes the layout, the colors, the fonts, and the usability.
  • We participate in either one-on-one client interaction during the design process or team client vision development.
  • Our web designer team constructs wireframes and prototypes to try out design concepts; provide feedback on visual elements such as logos, brands, and styles.

Web Developers:

  • Our web development teams build websites, creating the code that runs a website, whether it's client-side or server-side.
  • Our web developers bring to life the website design parameters from the design team.
  • Our development skills include being the best at a variety of programming languages, including HTML (HyperText Markup Languages), CSS, PHP, and JavaScript but also extending to other languages (Ruby, C/C++, Python), frameworks, and libraries.

For more on this topic, read our article about all the differences between web development and web design.

Which language is best for web development?

There is no single best language for web development. Our team works closely with you to determine the best languages for your web development based on the requirements and goals of the project we’re doing for you and the types of web solutions we’re implementing.

For example, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript are essential for front-end development and form the basis of most websites or web applications.

On the back-end web development side, languages like Python, PHP, Ruby, Java, and Node.js each have their strengths. Python is known for its readability and versatility, while PHP is popular for its extensive support and ease of use.

What is a web developer's job?

The job of a web developer is to build and maintain websites. They are also in charge of the site's technical details, such as its speed and its ability to deal with a high volume of visitors.

What are HTML tables and forms?

To fully grasp the fundamentals of HTML coding, you must first understand the three building blocks, which are:

  1. Tags
  2. Elements
  3. Attributes

HTML tables are a method of organizing and presenting data on a web page in a structured, grid-like format. Tables are made up of rows and columns, which form cells that can hold data, text, or images.

HTML forms are essential components of all websites, allowing you to input and submit data. Text fields, checkboxes, radio buttons, dropdown menus, and submission buttons are common input elements in forms. They facilitate user interactions by enabling tasks such as account registration, data collection, and search queries, ultimately improving a website's user experience and functionality.

JavaScript frameworks are pre-built JavaScript libraries and developer tools that make web application development easier and faster. They provide reusable code, established patterns, and robust functionality, allowing developers to more efficiently build complex, feature-rich applications.
