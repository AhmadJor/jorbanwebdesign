How much does a SaaS website cost?

 

The cost to build a SaaS website can vary greatly depending on the complexity of the software, the features and functionality required, the level of customization, and the experience of the development team. A basic SaaS website could cost anywhere from $25,000 to $75,000, while a more complex and feature-rich platform could cost upwards of $200,000 or more.

It is important to work with a reputable and experienced development team that can provide a detailed proposal outlining the project, the scope of work and associated costs. It is also important to consider ongoing maintenance and support costs, as well as any additional fees for hosting and infrastructure.

Overall, the cost to build a SaaS website should be viewed as an investment in the long-term success of the business.

To ensure your SaaS website design is optimized for success, it's essential to follow best practices such as clear messaging, intuitive navigation, mobile responsiveness, and strong calls-to-action.

To effectively communicate the value of a SaaS web design agency's services, clear and concise messaging is key. Marketing managers understand that their time is valuable, and they need to quickly grasp the benefits that a SaaS web design agency can bring to their business.

Intuitive Navigation

Intuitive navigation is a key aspect of SaaS web design. It refers to the ease with which users can explore and interact with a website.

At Jorban, we prioritize intuitive navigation in all our design projects. This means carefully considering how users will navigate through the site, using clear labels and menu structures that make sense for your specific industry or target audience.

To achieve this level of user-friendliness, we rely on best practices like usability testing and data-driven decision-making.

Ultimately, intuitive navigation is critical for driving traffic and keeping visitors engaged with your brand online.

 

Mobile Responsiveness

In today's world, where over 50% of internet traffic comes from mobile devices, having a website that is optimized for mobile use is crucial. This means that your SaaS web design agency needs to prioritize building responsive websites that can adapt to different screen sizes and resolutions seamlessly.

Mobile responsiveness should be considered a best practice not only because it makes your site more accessible to users but also because Google prioritizes mobile-friendly websites in search rankings.

For example, Jorbanensures all their custom SaaS web designs are fully responsive across all platforms by using tools such as Webflow templates and Figma cloneables.

By doing so, they provide an exceptional user experience regardless of device type or size while maintaining the site's functionality and aesthetic appeal across multiple devices.

 

Strong Call-to-Action

One of the most crucial elements of SaaS web design is a strong call-to-action (CTA). A CTA prompts visitors to take desired actions on your website, such as signing up for a free trial or subscribing to your newsletter.

It's essential to incorporate CTAs that stand out and encourage users to complete an action.

A great example of a SaaS company with strong CTAs is Slack. Their homepage features multiple compelling CTAs that entice users to sign up for their platform's free version or upgrade to one of their paid plans.

They use language like "Create a new workspace" or "Join our community" and present them in bold colors against white space, making them easy to spot and click.

How do SaaS design agencies structure their pricing?

 

There are three main types of pricing models for a SaaS website project: fixed project cost, monthly retainer, and performance-based pricing.

 

Fixed Project Cost

The fixed project cost model is when the client pays a set price for the entire project, regardless of how long it takes to complete. This model is good for clients who have a clear idea of what they want and don't anticipate many changes during the project.

 

Monthly Retainer

The monthly retainer model is when the client pays a set fee each month for ongoing work and support. This model is good for clients who anticipate changes and updates to the site over time.

 

Performance-Based Pricing

Finally, performance-based pricing is when the client pays based on the success of great content on the website, such as the number of leads generated or the amount of revenue generated. This model is good for clients who want to ensure they are getting a return on their investment.

 

Each pricing model has its advantages and disadvantages, and it's important to choose the one that suits the client's specific needs and budget.
