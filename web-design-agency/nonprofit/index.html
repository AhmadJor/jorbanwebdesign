What are the differences between Web Design, Website Redesign, and Website Refresh?

 

Web design involves all aspects of your site’s user experience, aesthetic, and front-end functionality. Website design can be applied to a new site or an existing one. When applied to an existing website, we refer to it as a “website redesign." Website redesign is thus a subset of website design.

The terms “website redesign” and “website refresh” are often used synonymously, but we draw a distinction between the two.

  • Website redesign: an in-depth change to multiple aspects of the site, resulting in substantial differences to the website's appearance, user experience, performance, and underlying code.
  • Website refresh: a refresh is more cosmetic in nature than a redesign and could include updated fonts, an updated color scheme, limited user experience updates (for example, a button that gets a new shape), and other minor improvements.

Can you outline the steps involved in your web design process?

 

When you engage with Jorban, you’re embarking on a collaborative journey. In our initial meeting, we'll discuss your business objectives and website vision. Together, we'll develop a tailored web design strategy focused on driving leads and sales for your enterprise.

Here are the stages of our website design process:

  1. Pre-kickoff. We’ll get to know each other, and begin to understand your target audience and their pain points so we can create effective graphic design, visual elements, and content that will capture their attention and inspire action. We’ll also learn more about your brand, business, content management system, and technical requirements.
  2. Kickoff. The official start of the project; we’ll define all elements of the project scope and hold a workshop to define success, work on goal identification, and start thinking through structure, design, and development.
  3. Discovery. This is a critical step of web design. We’re digging deeper, combining notes from the kickoff with our own research and analytics. We build elements like personas, sitemap, and wireframe, as well as other visual design documents. We’ll also set up weekly status meetings with you.
  4. Creative. We make sure we’re on the same page creatively through mood boards and concept designs. We then take those findings to build out prototypes. In this phase of the web design process we also design the website’s real-life interface.
  5. Development. Time to start coding and actually build the website! In the development phase of the website project, you can expect work on the front-end, back-end, and content management systems. Regular check-ins and early access to a staging server allow us to stay in sync with you.
  6. Pre-launch. We’re getting ready for the big day. That means extended testing of all features, bug fixes, fixing broken links, and other tweaks to make sure it’s just right. We’ll also schedule the launch and handover date with you.
  7. Launch. On the day of the launch, our website design and development project team is available to work on any last-minute fixes and adjustments. By the end of the day, you have a fully functioning, beautiful new website.
  8. Post-launch. We’ll work for at least 10 business days with you to fix any bugs or broken links, and to make routine tweaks. We’ll also chat with you on potential long-term engagements for ongoing support.

What Determines the Price of a Website Design?

 

When it comes to getting a custom quote for professional website design services, there are several factors that play into the price.

The cost of a website design is based on the following factors:

  • The website's complexity. A more complex website with custom functionalities and features will typically take more time and effort to design.
  • The number of pages. The more pages a website has, the more time and effort each page will require to design.
  • The level of customization. A fully customized website with unique layouts, graphics, and branding elements will be more expensive than a template-based design.
  • The timeframe for completion. A shorter deadline for completing the website design can require your designers to work longer hours, which can increase the cost of the project.
  • Additional services. Services like website hosting, video production, SEO, and ongoing maintenance can add to the overall cost of your website design project.

Multiple landing pages with complex navigation may require more design or coding time than creating only a few simple brochure-style pages.

Additional touches are good to help attract and convert site visitors: good website copywriting, photography, app development, and search engine optimization.

When creating digital experiences, the ultimate goal should be optimizing for both performance and usability. While a CMS can assist in keeping the site organized and easy to maintain, ensuring it is optimized for search engines (national or local SEO) adds even more time and effort. All of these components come together to form the final cost for your website development project.
