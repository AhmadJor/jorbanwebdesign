Website Maintenance Costs

The costs of website maintenance can vary quite a lot depending on the following:

  • Website size and complexity. Small service business sites are simpler to maintain than massive ecommerce websites with thousands of products, for example.
  • Frequency of updates. Websites that need to be updated or have their content changed often may need more maintenance time and resources, which can lead to a higher maintenance cost.
  • Technological complexity. Websites with complicated technical features or those requiring specialist skills for upkeep may have higher maintenance expenses.
  • Security. Websites that need a high level of security may need more security measures or security updates more often, which will cost more to maintain.
  • Backup frequency. Websites that need to be backed daily may cost more to maintain than those being backed up on a monthly basis.

Our Website Maintenance Checklist

Here's a website maintenance checklist we use. Feel free to borrow it (you’re welcome!). Or, just hire us to help you keep your site working smoothly.

  1. Check website functionality. Test your website on a regular basis to check that all features, links, and forms are working properly.
  2. Website software updates. Regularly update the software on your website to guarantee it runs smoothly and safely.
  3. Website hosting. Make sure your web host is reputable and gives you all the tools you need for site security, site speed, and other performance metrics.
  4. Audit the website's load time. To improve user experience, monitor website speed and optimize for faster loading times.
  5. Backup website data on a regular basis to guarantee that data is secure and can be restored in the event of a website failure.
  6. Keep an eye on website security. Install security steps to safeguard your website from cyber threats and monitor for any security issues and security patches on a regular basis.
  7. Stay abreast of your website's contents. Regularly review and update website content to ensure it is correct, relevant, and up to date. Make sure your content management system is configured correctly.
  8. Evaluate website data to monitor traffic, user activity, and engagement in order to inform changes and optimizations.
  9. Optimize for mobile. Make sure your website is mobile-friendly in order to deliver a consistent user experience across all devices.
  10. Audit site links. Review and update your website’s internal and external links on a regular basis to verify they are functional and not broken. Fix any broken links you find.
  11. Evaluate and update website design on a regular basis to ensure it is modern, user-friendly, and interesting.
  12. Ensure any website development is performed in a scalable way consistent with your business goals.

By following this website maintenance checklist on a regular basis, you can ensure that your website is working smoothly and offering an excellent user experience for your visitors. Website Support FAQ

Who normally maintains a website?

Many companies have a full-time website manager or IT department in charge of website upkeep. Outsourcing website maintenance to a professional maintenance provider can be a good option for companies that don’t have a fulltime in-house role. Often, these companies delegate site maintenance to a marketing manager or social media specialist, but these roles often don’t have the expertise to manage a website.

For some business owners, outsourcing website maintenance can be a cost-effective choice because it avoids the overhead costs involved with hiring a full-time website owner or IT staff member. 

Is maintaining a website difficult?

Aspects of maintaining your website can be difficult, depending on the nature of the site and the resources at your disposal. In general, website maintenance includes updating the site regularly to make sure it runs smoothly, safely, and efficiently.

Some parts of managing a website, like changing the content, optimizing the graphics, or making new pages, can be very easy. For more difficult tasks, like updating software, adding security patches, or keeping a database up to date, you may need more specific knowledge and skills.

Website upkeep can be especially difficult for firms that do not have a dedicated webmaster or IT team. In these situations, hiring a professional website maintenance company can be a good idea. This lets businesses take advantage of the experience of people who are dedicated to making sure their website is always up to date and running at its best.

What is a website maintenance contract?

A website maintenance contract is an agreement between a website owner and a website maintenance provider that spells out what services will be done and how they will be paid for.
