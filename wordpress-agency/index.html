What Is WordPress?

WordPress is an incredibly powerful and reliable open-source content management system that is licensed under the GNU General Public License version 2 (GPLv2). It is an easy-to-use platform for creating and managing websites, blogs, and applications. With WordPress, users can create dynamic websites that are quick to set up and manage.

Anyone can use or modify the WordPress software for free, giving developers the ability to customize their websites without having to pay for expensive services.

With WordPress, you can create:

  • Corporate websites
  • Blogs
  • Ecommerce stores
  • Forums
  • Membership sites

…and more

WordPress is a great solution for any company looking for flexibility, scalability, and minimal cost.

Enjoy our ultimate guide to the WordPress platform!

Reasons For Using WordPress

WordPress is the world’s most popular content management system (CMS). It’s great for web design and development because of its user-friendly interface, incredible versatility, and security features. It can be used for almost any project, from simple blogs to complex websites. Powerful themes and plugins let users design and manage their own content and website. It comes with built-in SEO features that let you improve your website's online visibility and reach more people.

WordPress sites are great for a wide range of companies, from enterprise organizations to small local businesses.

There are many advantages to using WordPress as a website platform. Here are some of the top reasons why people choose WordPress over other platforms:

  • Free and open source. Anyone can access WordPress, modify it and use it as they wish. There are no upfront fees or restrictions on what you can do with WordPress, making it ideal for website developers and companies that value open-ended possibilities.
  • Extensible. Business owners can modify the platform with themes and plugins. It’s easy to customize your WordPress business website. From creating custom plugins to building your own theme (as we’ve done with our custom WordPress theme for our clients), you can extend the functionality of your website endlessly. You can create your own contact forms, set up an ecommerce store with WooCommerce, and do pretty much whatever you want..
  • Easy to manage. It doesn’t take long to get comfortable with how WordPress works. Even beginners can make their way around the back-end dashboard quickly. Plus, there are lots of tutorials and blogs available to help new users get started with their websites.
  • Flexible. Whether you want a B2B website or an ecommerce store, WordPress can accommodate all different types of sites. It’s highly configurable so you have full control over how your site looks and functions.
  • Mobile responsive. With more people browsing the web on mobile devices these days, having a mobile-friendly website is essential. Fortunately, most WordPress themes come fully responsive so they will look great regardless of what device someone views them on.
  • Built-in blogging platform. A blog is one of the most important tools for any business or organization. Luckily, WordPress has a built-in blogging platform so you don't need any additional software installed on your server for creating posts or managing comments from readers. This makes running a successful blog much easier than ever before!
  • 24/7 support. WordPress offers live chat customer support for their website subscribers with either a Premium or plugin-enabled plan.

Whether your website requires sophisticated user authentication procedures, regularly updated content libraries, or large databases that need secure access, WordPress provides the power and flexibility needed to make sure everything runs smoothly.

WordPress Project Plan

It is important to have a plan when undertaking a WordPress project. A WordPress project plan makes sure that the goals of WordPress design and development are clear, that tasks are assigned and kept track of, and that everyone involved in the project is kept up to date.

Here are some of the issues that our redesign team focuses on before launching a WordPress website redesign:

  1. Website performance. We will do a comprehensive audit of your current website to identify any potential issues that could impede performance or lead to a drop in conversions. This includes testing page load times, ensuring images are properly optimized, and the site is responsive across all devices.
  2. Website conversions. We will conduct A/B testing to determine which page elements may lead to higher conversion rates; this includes button colors, headlines, copywriting, and imagery.
  3. Site structure. We ensure the site is easily navigable and structured with an intuitive architecture that makes sense to visitors and algorithms alike.
  4. Color palette. Colors will be chosen to complement the brand's identity while also reflecting customer preferences as determined by A/B testing experiments or surveys.
  5. Bounce rates. Reducing bounce rate is top priority. We optimize page loading speed and make sure the overall user experience is good so that visitors stay on your site and take a desired action.
  6. Authority and credibility. It’s important for websites to display credentials such as awards won or certifications held by the company along with a customer reviews section where past clients can post their experiences. This will help persuade prospective buyers who might still doubt finalizing their order due lack of information.

WordPress Conversion Rate Optimization

Conversion rate is hugely important for your business, and we’ve made a science of conversion rate optimization. We can help you turn your WordPress website into a conversion machine.

Your conversion rate is the number of conversions divided by the number of visitors, multiplied by 100. If you get 100 visitors, and out of those you get 4 purchases, your conversion rate is 4%. The conversion rate is a key metric for businesses to track, as it indicates how well they are doing at turning web traffic into paying customers.

There are a number of factors that can influence conversion rate, such as the website’s design, the quality of your product, shipping, and your pricing. Conversion rates can be optimized by using A/B tests for different versions of the website and by providing users with an incentive to convert, such as a discount or free shipping.

Our team of experts knows how to make your Wordpress website get more visits and more conversions. We look at your site’s performance data to make a custom plan to help you get the most out of every site visit.

With our CRO services, you'll see measurable improvements in your site's performance, such as more conversions, more sales, and happier customers. And because we continue to monitor and improve your site, you can be sure that it will continue to perform.

WordPress Agency FAQ

What is a WordPress agency?

A WordPress agency is a company that specializes in using the WordPress platform to design, build, and manage websites.

A WordPress agency may offer a wide range of services, including website hosting, custom design and development, retail ecommerce solutions, content management systems, plugin integration, and analytics tracking.

WordPress agencies work with clients to make sure their unique business needs are met by providing customized services that are tailored to their goals and budget.

How do I choose a WordPress agency?

Research agencies to make sure they have the relevant expertise for your project. Look for completed projects in their portfolio that match your goals and objectives.

Look for good reviews from customers and approval from other groups. Determine the approach of the agency by gaining insights into how they take on new projects, keeping in mind their size, availability, and timeline estimates.

Lastly, before giving the go-ahead on any project, understand what role will be played by your business in bringing the project to a successful conclusion.

Is WP Engine the same as WordPress?

WP Engine is a web hosting solution that is specifically tailored to the WordPress platform. It is also a crowd favorite WordPress hosting solution and provides tailored services specifically for the WordPress platform. These include API integration, automated updates and improved loading speeds.

The web server software itself is the same between WP Engine and WordPress; however, WP Engine supplies a suite of tools optimized for WordPress sites. This includes using the latest versions of PHP and MySQL, which further increase performance and security. Whether you're looking to build a website from scratch or simply want more control over an existing project, WP Engine can provide a tailor-fit solution that is backed by 24/7 customer support.

There are also many other great cloud hosting solutions for WordPress sites.

What are the most useful plugins for WordPress?

WordPress is an incredibly useful platform for website creation and management, but it is made even more powerful by the utilization of plugins.

  • One of the most popular and beneficial plugins for WordPress is the website builder Elementor, which helps with page building and design customization.
  • Yoast SEO helps webmasters increase their website's visibility in search engines and optimize content for greater audience reach.
  • Akismet is also a valuable plugin, protecting websites from malicious comments left by spammers.
  • For collecting leads and emails, there's Gravity Forms. It provides a user-friendly interface for collecting data from customers.
  • For WordPress booking, specialized plugins like TeamBooking, WooCommerce Bookings, or BirchPress Scheduler are helpful for creating appointment booking pages on any WordPress site.
  • WP Rocket can speed up loading times and lower server costs to improve the performance of a website.
  • Imagify is also a great plugin for compressing images to make pages load faster without sacrificing quality.

There are also booking plugins, plugins to manage your events calendar, plugins to auto-stream your social media feed onto your site, and many other types.

Is there a headless WordPress solution?

A headless WordPress site decouples the frontend user experience from content management. It uses WordPress as a content management system (CMS) only; the visual presentation of the content is done via some other system.

Is WordPress good for client management?

If you’re looking to manage client interactions, you might want to invest in customer relationship management (CRM) software like HubSpot or Zoho CRM.

Can I build an online store on WordPress?

Yes, with WooCommerce, you can manage your ecommerce business on WordPress. WooCommerce is a powerful ecommerce solution that sits atop the WordPress platform and adds shopping cart functionality and other essential ecommerce capabilities.

What does WordPress Multisite do?

WordPress Multisite is a powerful WordPress feature that allows users to create and manage multiple WordPress sites from a single WordPress installation. It provides a shared user base, which enables user authentication across multiple WordPress sites. It also allows site administrators to manage installations from one WordPress dashboard and customize the network of websites with common settings, themes, plugins, and more.

WordPress Multisite is particularly useful for WordPress users looking for an easier way to create and manage multiple WordPress sites and networks of websites, for either business or personal use.

There are also booking plugins, scheduling plugins, and all sorts of other handy WordPress plugins.
